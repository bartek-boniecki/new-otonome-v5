Process

How a Sprint runs.

Eight weeks, fixed scope. Every phase ties to a public artifact — nothing about the method is proprietary.

1 · Attribution wired end-to-end.

Read-only access to your gateway and provider accounts. Every call mapped to a feature, cohort, environment. We use your existing tools.

→ backed by the audit checklist · github.com/basis-finops

2 · Top 3 leaks. Written. Ranked.

By end of week 2, a written diagnosis: where the spend is, what's recoverable, what the fix is, what it'll save. If we can't find 3× the fee, we stop here at no cost.

→ priced against cost-per-token tracker · every provider, weekly

3 · Routing & caching shipped.

Code lands in your repo with your review. Evals run before and after every change. Quality must hold within tolerance bands you set in week 1, or the change is reverted.

→ reference past teardowns · typical fix patterns

4 · Task model trained — if it pays back.

Only when routing surfaced a workload with both volume and a narrow task definition. Often skipped. We're honest when this isn't the right move.

→ verified by eval suite in CI · your team, going forward

5 · Make it your team's, not ours.

Runbooks, on-call docs, eval suite checked into CI. 30-day check-in included. Your team extends the work. We don't engineer dependency.

→ output verified savings report · signed off, reproducible

FAQ

Answered straight.

Not marketing FAQ. Questions every prospect actually asks on call one.

We tried this internally for two weeks and gave up. What's different?+

Internal attempts usually stall at attribution — the gateway isn't emitting per-feature telemetry, so every optimization is a guess. We arrive with the instrumentation checklist, a per-provider price tracker, and an eval harness already built. Your engineers keep shipping product; we do the part that keeps getting deprioritized.

How do you prove the savings aren't just attribution shifting?+

Baseline is frozen in week 1 in your own billing data, per route, with traffic volume held constant in the model. Every saving in the final report is reproducible from your provider invoices — not our dashboard. If a number can't be recomputed by your finance team, it doesn't count toward the 3×.

What happens when you leave?+

Everything lives in your repo and your tools: dashboards, routing code, cache config, runbooks, and the eval suite in CI. There's no basis agent running in your stack and no license to renew. A 30-day check-in is included; after that your team extends the work — that's the design goal, not a concession.

Our spend is "only" $10–20k/month. Is this worth it?+

At $12k/month, a mid-range 35% cut is roughly $50k/year — which clears the 3× bar on the low end of the Sprint fee. If the audit shows it won't clear, we say so in writing and stop there. That's exactly what the free audit is for.

The audit is free. The diagnosis is written. The decision is yours.
